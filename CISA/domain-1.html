Domain 1: Information System Auditing Process

Part A: Planning

Purpose of Audits

Audits serve multiple purposes, including ensuring effective operations, compliance, readiness for future challenges, and the safeguarding of information assets. IS audits specifically focus on the information systems that support business operations and competitive advantage.

Definition and Objectives

IS auditing is a formal process that evaluates whether systems comply with laws, regulations, governance policies, and whether they meet confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) expectations.

Typical Audit Process

  • Planning
  • Fieldwork/Documentation
  • Reporting/Follow-up

These phases may be broken down further and adapted to suit audit standards such as ISACAâ€™s ITAF.

IS Audit Standards & Guidelines

ISACA provides standards that define mandatory requirements and guidelines for IS audit practices, including tools, techniques, and professional expectations.

  • General: Ethics, objectivity, and competency
  • Performance: Planning, scoping, supervision
  • Reporting: Communication methods and content

ISACA Code of Professional Ethics

Guides the conduct of professionals, including expectations on objectivity, confidentiality, competence, and reporting transparency.

ITAF Framework

The IT Assurance Framework provides comprehensive guidance for conducting audits and sets expectations for auditor performance, reporting, and responsibility.

IS Internal Audit Function

Outlined by an audit charter approved by leadership, defining scope, authority, consulting roles, and ensuring independence and objectivity.

Audit Resource Management

Maintaining up-to-date skills, using CAATs, and planning annual training aligned with tech trends is essential for auditor effectiveness.

Outsourcing and Use of Experts

Audit teams may use external experts for specialized areas. Proper supervision, defined contracts, and review processes are vital to maintain quality and compliance.

Types of Audits

  • IS Audit
  • Compliance Audit
  • Financial Audit
  • Operational Audit
  • Integrated Audit
  • Fraud and Forensic Audits
  • Third-party Service Audits

Each audit type serves different objectives, from compliance to fraud detection to operational efficiency.
